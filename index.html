ALLY KLINE'S GIRLS WHO CODE PORTFOLIO

SO, WHAT'S THIS PORTFOLIO ABOUT?

This is a little portfolio of the projects I did during the Girls Who Code 2016 Summer Immersion program. Feel free to take a peek at some of my work! (Click on a tab to see some of the work I did that week.)

WEEK 1: SCRATCH

Scratch is a visual programming language. We used Scratch during the first week of the Girls who Code program to get a feel for computer programming. Below are two projects that I worked on in Scratch: Pig and the Jukebox. Clicking on the images will bring you to the two programs on the Scratch website, where you can see them in action alongside descriptions of what they do.

WEEK 2: PYTHON

During the second week of Girls Who Code, we worked with Python and created Mad Libs, a text adventure game, a random name generator, a grocery list creator. We also learned how to hash and store passwords.

WEEK 3: PYGAME

During Week 3, we worked with the Python library, Pygame. I found it particularily difficult, but I somehow managed. We made bouncing balls, snow fall, and a city scroller.

WEEK 4: ARDUINO ROBOTICS

This is a video of my group's Arduino UNO BoEBot dancing to the song "Bet on It" from High School Musical 2. During this portion of the program, we also wired LEDs to blink, programmed Piezo songs, and used the same robot as a whisker bot to detect walls and other obstacles.

WEEK 5: WEB DEVELOPMENT

During Week 5, we focused on Web Development. During this week we learned HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, and JavaScript/jQuery. I made this portfolio and another website (link below :) while learning the three languages and two libraries.

WHO IS THIS ALLY KLINE PERSON, ANYWAYS?
